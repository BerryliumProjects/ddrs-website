Hi Fun - March 2017

John Kelly, G3YGG

HF conditions in March were fairly average for this stage in the sun spot cycle, but a number of big and popular contests proved that DX is still relatively easy - perhaps because more stations transmit in these. It is too easy at other times - and fatal - to tune over an HF band, hear nothing, and then switch off. We should all, at least, give two or three CQ calls before doing so.

Whilst I do not take contests seriously I like to have a few QSOs and in the ARRL SSB contest I enjoyed working a few W's on 80 metres (split frequency), and on 40 metres. On 20 metres, with only 5 watts, I exchanged reports with Canada and Stateside. 15 metres was open too, during daylight hours.

I could not spend much time on the air during the 24 hours of the BERU Contest but worked ZB2, 9H1, C4, and 9G on CW quickly on the Sunday morning.

Then, later in the month, before I left for a /P holiday in Cyprus, I worked an all time new one: ZX5J in the South Shetlands. So not a bad month.

And Keith reports that he worked W7 on SSB with his 50 watts and vertical antenna.

In Cyprus H2017PFO is on air every Tuesday morning on 20 and 15 metres before 0900Z. Do give them a call. They have two new female licencees in their club.
